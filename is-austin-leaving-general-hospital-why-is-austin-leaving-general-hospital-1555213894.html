General Hospital

"General Hospital" is a long-running American soap opera that first premiered on April 1, 1963, on the ABC television network. Created by Frank and Doris Hursley, the show started with stories centered around the seventh floor of General Hospital in an unnamed midsize Eastern city. Over the years, it evolved and became known for its engaging storylines, featuring characters like Steve Hardy (played by John Beradino) and Nurse Jessie Brewer (played by Emily McLaughlin). The show expanded from 30 minutes to a full hour in 1978 and has since become the longest-running entertainment program in ABC television history.

In the late 1970s, the storyline focus shifted to the Quartermaine and Spencer families. Notably, the show reached its peak in the early 1980s with the iconic "supercouple" Luke and Laura, whose 1981 wedding remains the highest-rated hour in American soap opera history. "General Hospital" has received numerous Daytime Emmy Awards and continues to be a beloved part of American television, celebrating its 50th anniversary in 2013 and airing its 15,000th episode on June 22, 2022.

General Hospital Release Date

"General Hospital" made its debut on April 1, 1963, and has been a staple of American daytime television ever since. Premiering on the ABC television network, the soap opera quickly became a household name. It holds the Guinness World Record for the longest-running American soap opera in production and the second-longest in American history. The show's success has also contributed to its recognition as the world's third longest-running scripted drama series in production, following British serials The Archers and Coronation Street.

Throughout its extensive history, "General Hospital" has undergone various changes, expanding from 30 minutes to 45 minutes in 1976 and eventually to a full hour on January 16, 1978. Despite the challenges faced by soap operas in the changing television landscape, "General Hospital" has persisted, becoming the oldest American soap opera on September 17, 2010. It remains a cultural phenomenon, celebrated for its compelling storylines and enduring popularity.
