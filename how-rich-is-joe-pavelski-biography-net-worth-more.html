How rich is Joe Pavelski: Biography, Net Worth &amp; more

Joe Pavelski is an American professional ice hockey player who has amassed a net worth in excess of US$30 million as of 2020, according to Sportskeeda. His primary source of income stems from his career in the National Hockey League (NHL) which spans over sixteen seasons.

Who is Joe Pavelski?

Born Joseph James Pavelski on July 11, 1984 in Plover, Wisconsin, United States. He is one of four children birthed to Mike and Sandy Pavelski. His other siblings are Sheri Pavelski, Jerry Pavelski, and Scott Pavelski.

He attended the University of Wisconsin and played for the Wisconsin Badgers men’s ice hockey team.

In 2002, he won a Wisconsin State Hockey Championship with SPASH (Stevens Point Area Senior High) Panthers. He became a member of the 2004 Clark Cup champion Waterloo Black Hawks of the USHL and won the 2004 USHL Dave Tyler Junior Player of the Year Award.

During his collegiate years, he played over two seasons (2004-2006) at the University of Wisconsin of the Western Collegiate Hockey Association, where he recorded 101 points (39–62) in 84 games.

He led his team to the 2006 NCAA Division I Men’s Ice Hockey Championship as the leader in terms of overall points. Prior to winning the NCAA championship, Pavelski’s performances earned him a call-up to the All-WCHA Rookie Team in 2005, was selected to the All-WCHA Second Team, and the Men’s RBK Division I West All-America Second Team.

Pavelski began his NHL career in the 2006–07 season. He began on a hot streak, scoring a goal in his first game, and scoring 7 goals and 10 points in his first 12 games. He earned the nickname “Little Joe” from Sharks announcer Randy Hahn, a reference to Pavelski’s teammate and San Jose superstar “Jumbo Joe”, Joe Thornton.
