Is the addressee the sender or the receiver

Is a addressee the sender?

As nouns the difference between addressee and sender

is that addressee is the person or organization to which something, such as a letter or message, is addressed or sent, for whom the item is intended while sender is someone who sends.

What does name of addressee mean?

addressee. / (ˌædrɛˈsiː) / noun. a person or organization to whom a letter, parcel, etc, is addressed. a person who is addressed in conversation, a speech, a poem, etc.

Who is the addressee receiver?

An addressee is the person a letter or email is addressed to (who you are sending it to). A recipient is the person who receives the letter or email (or the person who will receive it).

Who is addresser and addressee?

What is root word of addressee?

Is addressee and receiver same?

As nouns the difference between receiver and addressee

is that receiver is a person who or thing that receives or is intended to receive something while addressee is the person or organization to which something, such as a letter or message, is addressed or sent, for whom the item is intended.
