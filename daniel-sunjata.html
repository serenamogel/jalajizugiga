Daniel Sunjata is an American actor who is famous for playing the role of a sailor on shore leave on the first post-9/11 themed episode of Sex and the City. He received a Tony Award nomination for his work in Take Me Out. He rose to prominence for his famous roles in shows like Rescue Me at FX, The Bronx is Burning, Graceland, and many more. He has also appeared in films like “The Dark Knight Rises” and “The Devil Wears Prada.” In 2004, he portrayed poet Langston Hughes in the film Brother to Brother. He won a Theatre World Award for his breakout Broadway performance as a gay Major League Baseball player, in 2003. He played “Nurse Eli” on the TV series Grey’s Anatomy, from 2010 to 2011. He starred as a main cast of series, ‘Power Book II: Ghost’ which was renewed for a third season which premiered on March 17, 2023. Also, in January 2023, ahead of the third season premiere, the series was renewed for a fourth season.

Daniel Sunjata Age and Parents

Daniel Sunjata was born on December 30, 1971 in Evanston, IL. He is 51 years old, as of 2023. His zodiac sign is Capricorn. His parents had careers of a police dispatcher and civil rights worker. He was born and raised in Evanston, Illinois, a suburb north of Chicago. In 2003, he received a Theater World award. He is of African, German, and Irish descent. His adoptive parents are of Irish and Italian-German descent. Daniel Sunjata is the adopted son of Bill and Catherine Condon a police dispatcher and a civil rights worker.

Daniel Sunjata Education

Daniel Sunjata attended Mount Carmel High School, Chicago (played linebacker for two state championship football teams). Then, he went to Florida A&M University. After this, he attended University of Louisiana at Lafayette (Bachelor of Arts degree). He also went to New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts (Master of Fine Arts).
