How did Donatella Versace start her Professional Career?

Donatella Versace embarked on her fashion industry career after accompanying her brother Gianni Versace on an educational journey to Italy, where she pursued intricate knitwear design. The release of “Blonde,” a perfume named after her, earned her her label, “Versus” by Gianni.

In the wake of her brother’s passing, Versace inaugurated her inaugural Haute couture exhibition for the Versace Atelier at the “Hotel Ritz Paris” on July 18, 1998.

Versace’s career took off in 2000 when she designed the Green Versace dress (Jungle Dress) for Jennifer Lopez to wear to the 42nd Grammy Awards.

In September 2000, she began work on the Palazzo Versace Australia resort, located on the Gold Coast in Queensland, Australia.

The most renowned Versace garments designed by Gianni and Donatella Versace were on exhibit at the Victoria and Albert Museum in London in 2002.

In 2008, she assumed the role of honorary chairman of the “Fashion Fringe” in London. The FGI Superstar Award was bestowed upon her in the same year.

Under her direction, “Versace” expanded its product offerings to encompass household furnishings and accessories.

Donatella was named “Fashion Designer of the Year” by Glamour magazine in 2012 and 2016, and “Women of the Year” in 2010.

Lady Gaga composed “Donatella” for Donatella Versace, which appeared on her 2013 album Artpop.

The Fashion Awards in 2017 bestowed upon her the prestigious honor of “Fashion Icon of the Year.”

Concurrently, she received the “Fashion Icon” and the “International CFDA Award” at The Green Carpet Fashion Awards in the same year.

The documentary “House of Versace,” which premiered on Lifetime Network in 2013, portrays actual occurrences within the Versace family.

Despite the 2018 sale of Versace to Capri Holdings, Donatella continues to serve as the organization’s CEO.
